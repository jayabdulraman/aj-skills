# Style DNA

## One Sentence

Pure white, minimal, hand-drawn, spacious, restrained, absurd, product-sketch-like, English handwritten, structurally clear without becoming an instruction manual.

It should feel like an explanatory sketch casually drawn on a blank sheet of paper by someone who has spent years thinking about AI, product, design, and developer tools.

## Must Have

- 16:9 horizontal inline article illustration.
- Pure white background: no beige, warm gray, paper texture, gradients, shadows, noise, or vintage paper feel.
- Mostly black hand-drawn line art: thin lines, slight wobble, not mechanical, not vector-clean, not heavy outlines.
- Generous whitespace: the main subject uses about 40%-60% of the canvas, with at least 35% blank white space and ideally one calm empty area.
- Sparse English handwritten notes: at most 5-8 notes, ideally 1-4 words each.
- One image explains one core action, structure, state, or metaphor.
- Let the structure read naturally; do not write the structure type on the image.

## Color

- Black: main line art, Xiaohei, frames, structure, main text, and primary objects.
- Red: emphasis notes, problems, emotional friction, key reminders, or results.
- Orange: main flow, paths, arrows, automation movement, and A-to-B transitions.
- Blue: secondary notes, mental state, system state, second-layer explanation, AI/assistant/automation hints.

Blue is not required in every image. Keep color restrained; use less rather than more.

## Never Do

- Do not make commercial illustration.
- Do not make a PPT infographic.
- Do not make a formal flowchart.
- Do not make a course slide.
- Do not make a cute cartoon poster.
- Do not make children's illustration.
- Do not make a complex architecture diagram.
- Do not make polished flat vector illustration.
- Do not make futuristic UI.
- Do not use real app screenshots.
- Do not use complex backgrounds, gradients, shadows, or texture.
- Do not explain every node.
- Do not write type labels such as "Workflow," "System Architecture," "Pitfalls," or "Roadmap" in the top-left corner.

## Aesthetic Direction

It should be absurd, inventive, interesting, simple, clean, and imaginative.

Avoid cute, childish, complex, stiff, or overly polished results.
